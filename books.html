Some of Our Books

O Sol é para Todos

Author: Harper Lee

Release Date: 1960

Genres: Novel, Drama, Classic Literature

Synopsis: Narrated by Scout Finch, a child from the southern United States in the 1930s, the book addresses racism and social injustice when her father, lawyer Atticus Finch, defends an unjustly accused black man.

A Doçura do Mundo

Author: Thrity Umrigar

Release Date: 2007

Genre: Contemporary Novel

Synopsis: Tehmina, a Parsi widow, moves from Bombay to the United States to live with her son and daughter-in-law. She faces challenges adapting to a new culture while trying to maintain her identity and values amidst generational and cultural differences.

A Teoria de Tudo

Author: Jane Hawking

Release Date: 2007

Genre: Biography, Autobiography

Synopsis: Stephen Hawking explains the concepts of cosmology, such as the Big Bang and black holes, and explores the search for a theory that unifies all physical phenomena of the universe.

Estranho Encontro

Author: Susanna Kearsley

Release Date: 2004

Genre: Historical Romance, Mystery

Synopsis: Sara, a cryptanalyst, investigates old diaries and connects with the history of Mary Dundas, a young woman from the 18th century. The plot blends historical mysteries and romances from the past and present.

Os Doze

Author: Justin Cronin

Release Date: 2012

Genre: Science Fiction, Post-apocalyptic

Synopsis: In this second book of the "The Passage" trilogy, the survivors of a viral pandemic face dangerous creatures and try to rebuild civilization while battling ongoing threats.

Fundamentos Filosóficos da Educação

Author: José Antônio Vasconcelos

Release Date: 2017

Genre: Philosophy of Education

Synopsis: José Antônio Vasconcelos explains how philosophy influences education, addressing different schools of thought. The book highlights the importance of philosophy in stimulating critical thinking and creativity in students.

Colapso

Author: Jared Diamond

Release Date: 2005

Genre: History, Sociology

Synopsis: Jared Diamond analyzes why some civilizations have disappeared, highlighting factors like environmental destruction and poor political decisions. The book provides lessons to avoid crises in current society.

O Despertar da Águia

Author: Leonardo Boff

Release Date: 1998

Genre: Philosophy, Spirituality

Synopsis: Leonardo Boff explores the concepts of "sim-bólico" (which unites) and "dia-bólico" (which separates) to analyze the construction of reality. The work aims to help the reader better understand themselves and the world, showing how opposing forces shape history and human life.

O Gerente-Minuto

Authors: Ken Blanchard and Spencer Johnson

Release Date: 1982

Genre: Management, Leadership

Synopsis: Ken Blanchard and Spencer Johnson present three simple techniques for effective leadership: setting clear goals, giving quick praise, and correcting mistakes objectively. The method improves productivity, engagement, and motivation at work.

Negritude, Cinema e Educação - Volume 2

Author: Edileuza Penha de Souza

Release Date: 2011

Genres: Law, Politics, and Sociology

Synopsis: Organized by Edileuza Penha de Souza, the book analyzes films and discusses themes like racism, Afro-Brazilian culture, and education. It serves as a guide for teachers, encouraging the application of Law 10.639/03 for more inclusive education.

Chapadão do Bugre

Author: Mário Palmério

Release Date: 1965

Genre: Regionalist Novel

Synopsis: Mário Palmério portrays life in the Brazilian backcountry, highlighting human relations, politics, and the challenges of rural communities.

Esmaguem Meu Coração

Author: João Gilberto Noll

Release Date: Release date unspecified

Genre: Contemporary Novel

Synopsis: João Gilberto Noll narrates the journey of a lonely protagonist, exploring identity, solitude, and the search for belonging.

Eu é um Outro

Author: Arthur Rimbaud

Release Date: Release date unspecified

Genre: French Poetry

Synopsis: Rimbaud explores the complexity of human identity, showing how each person is formed by different facets.

Coleção Descobrindo os Clássicos: Machado de Assis

Author: Machado de Assis

Release Date: Release date unspecified

Genre: Brazilian Literature

Synopsis: The collection presents summaries and analyses of the works of Machado de Assis, helping readers understand his themes and style.

O Coruja

Author: José Cândido de Carvalho

Release Date: Release date unspecified

Genre: Regionalist Novel

Synopsis: José Cândido de Carvalho tells the story of an introspective man in a small town, reflecting the local culture and customs.

Programming Language Processors in Java

Authors: David A. Watt and Deryck F. Brown

Release Year: 2000

Genre: Computing, Programming Languages

Synopsis: The book teaches how to create compilers and interpreters in Java, covering lexical analysis, syntax analysis, and code generation. It includes theory, examples, and practical exercises.

Seven Languages in Seven Weeks

Author: Bruce A. Tate

Release Year: 2010

Genre: Programming, Programming Languages

Synopsis: Bruce A. Tate explores seven programming languages, each with a different paradigm: Ruby, Io, Prolog, Scala, Erlang, Clojure, and Haskell. The book presents concepts, practical challenges, and analysis, helping the reader understand different approaches to programming.

Criando Páginas Web com CSS

Authors: Andy Budd, Cameron Moll, and Simon Collison

Release Year: 2006

Genre: Web Development, CSS

Synopsis: The book teaches advanced CSS techniques to create modern, responsive, and accessible websites. It covers best practices and web standards, helping developers enhance their skills.

Algoritmos e Lógica da Programação

Authors: Marco A. Furlan de Souza, Marcelo Marques Gomes, Marcio Vieira Soares, and Ricardo Concilio

Release Year: Not specified

Genre: Algorithms, Logic of Programming

Synopsis: The book teaches fundamental concepts of algorithms and logic of programming. It covers techniques for problem-solving and data structures. Ideal for beginners who want to improve their programming skills.

Web Services Security

Author: Mark O'Neill

Release Year: 2003

Genre: System Security, Web Services

Synopsis: Mark O'Neill teaches how to implement secure web services, addressing trust, confidentiality, and encryption. The book explains how to use security tokens for authentication and associate information such as name, password, identity, and privileges with messages.
